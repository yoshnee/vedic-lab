Core UI palette

Dark-only. A deep pine-green canvas layered up through surfaces to a single warm gold accent.

02

The Navagraha palette

Nine grahas, one palette. The seven sign-rulers are tuned to stay clearly distinct — they tint the houses of the chart. Rahu & Ketu, the shadow nodes, are muted and rule nothing.

7 ≠ 9

Twelve houses map to only seven ruler colors — Sun and Moon rule one sign each; Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus and Saturn rule two. So two houses sharing a color is expected, and meaningful. Rahu & Ketu get icon colors but no house tint.

03

The Navagraha, rendered

Not glyphs — modern celestial bodies. Each graha is a gradient-built sphere recolored to its palette assignment; the shadow nodes become what they actually are — Rahu the eclipse, Ketu the comet. They read at chart-cell size and hold up enlarged in detail panels.

04

Logo & mark

A combination mark. The symbol is the North Indian birth-chart grid — a square crossed by its diagonals with an inner diamond, the classic twelve-house layout — holding a single point of light where the diagonals meet. Jyotish means “science of light”; that gold spark is the soul of the mark. Short wordmark: Vedic Lab.

Sade Sati is the roughly seven-and-a-half year period when Saturn transits the signs adjacent to and over your natal Moon. The app derives it from Saturn's position over time — heavier to compute than a birth snapshot, and surfaced in the planet's expandable panel alongside its dignity and current dasha.

Palette in context

The system assembled: a North Indian chart (Aries ascendant, for demonstration) with houses tinted by their ruling planet's color, plus planet chips. This is a preview of the grid — not the full interactive build.

House coloring

Each house takes the color of the planet that rules its sign. Seven colors fill twelve houses.

07

Dignity states

A graha's strength is read from luminosity, halo and ring — never from its identity color. A debilitated Mars is still unmistakably red. The same visual language applies to all nine, so dignity reads at a glance regardless of planet. Rahu & Ketu stay neutral — nodal dignity is debated in the classical texts.
